One of England's Oldest National Parks, the Peak District, is a Fantastic Bouldering Destination

Things to Keep in Mind While Bouldering in the Peak District

Like many other regions, the increased popularity of bouldering in recent years unfortunately resulted in some ground erosion, particularly beneath popular bouldering sites. Employing bouldering mats can effectively mitigate this issue and is strongly recommended. Some areas have already lost their grass cover, and it's crucial to take preventive measures to avoid similar consequences elsewhere. Gritstone, being a relatively soft rock, is susceptible to damage once its surface layer wears away. This often occurs due to excessive brushing and chalk usage. Adhering to general best practices is essential: Use chalk sparingly. Always utilize bouldering mats. Stay on well-established paths. Gently brush holds using plastic brushes.
Embarking on your bouldering journey? Burbage South Valley provides an excellent starting point. If you've honed your skills indoors, this area is ideal for testing your outdoor capabilities for the first time. South Valley presents a variety of challenges and routes, all within a manageable height range to ensure secure landings. It's the perfect spot to boost your confidence in an outdoor setting.
Discussing the Peak District wouldn't be complete without highlighting the Stanage Area, particularly Stanage Plantation. Renowned for its array of classics, the gritstone boulders in Stanage Plantation stand out as the most famous in the UK. Nestled just beneath the Stanage Edge, it can be succinctly described as a field of problems reminiscent of The Pebble, known as the birthplace of bouldering in the district. The Plantation typically features higher graded problems from V5 and above, along with some more approachable cruxes to engage novice and intermediate boulderers.

Adhering to general best practices is essential:

  • Use chalk sparingly.
  • Always utilize bouldering mats.
  • Stay on well-established paths.
  • Gently brush holds using plastic brushes.

Which guide should I buy?

For a thorough exploration, consider consulting the Peak District Bouldering Guidebook authored by Rupert Davies and John Coefield. This guidebook provides an extensive overview of various crags, detailing what they have to offer, and encompasses over 3000 problems. Since the initial publication of the guidebook, problems and crags in the Peak District have undergone revisiting, reviewing, and revision. Davies and Coefield dedicated years to more extensive research, ensuring the guide reflects the latest information on both established and newly popular crags developed over the past six years.
