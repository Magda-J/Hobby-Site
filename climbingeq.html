Must Have Bouldering Equipment

1. Climbing Chalk

Climbing chalk consists primarily of powdered magnesium carbonate, serving climbers by preventing sweaty hands and enhancing grip on handholds. Various forms of bouldering chalk are accessible, such as loose chalk, chalk balls, chalk blocks, and liquid chalk. Experiment with these options to discover the climbing chalk that best suits your preferences.

2. Bouldering Chalk Bag

An indispensable gear item for bouldering is the climbing chalk bag. This is where you store your climbing chalk, aiding in keeping your hands dry and enhancing your grip on handholds. When selecting a chalk bag, take into account factors like size, shape, and closure type. We suggest opting for a bouldering bucket instead of a conventional climbing chalk bag, as they feature a broader and more stable base, enabling you to leave it on the ground while ascending the bouldering wall.

3. Climbing Shoes

Specialized footwear known as climbing shoes is crafted to offer the required grip and support for climbing activities. With various styles and designs available, it's crucial to select a pair that fits properly and is comfortable to wear. Explore our comprehensive guide on the top climbing shoes and determining the optimal tightness for climbing footwear.

4. Bouldering Mat

While unnecessary at an indoor bouldering wall, a bouldering mat becomes crucial for outdoor bouldering. Functioning to cushion your landing in case of a fall or slip, a high-quality bouldering crash pad acts as your safeguard against an unplanned encounter with the ground. Our Bouldering Pads are designed to connect together, forming a larger protective barrier to reduce the risk of injuries. When selecting crash pads, prioritize durability, portability, and effective cushioning.

5. Bouldering Brush

Utilized for clearing handholds of surplus chalk, dirt, and debris, a bouldering brush serves to enhance your grip and maintain the integrity of both rock and holds. Opt for a brush featuring soft bristles to avoid damaging holds or causing rock chips, which could impact the route for others. Boar hair brushes, being the most prevalent type of climbing brushes, prove effective on a variety of hold types.
